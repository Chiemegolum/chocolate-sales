# 🍫Chocolate Sales Performance

## 🔍Project Overview
An exploratory analysis and visualization of a 2-year sales data across 6 brands and 25 chocolate types to uncover revenue, customer demographic behavior, loyalty, and product performance. The project includes a well-detailed exploratory data analysis (EDA) in MySQL Workbench using comments, and an interactive Power BI dashboard.

## ⏳Project Goal
- To clean and prepare the 6 tables datasets for analysis and explore the datasets to find any possible meaningful insight to make informed decisions using SQL. 
- To build a dashboard to identify which chocolates and brands sell best and how revenue vary by store type, country, and time, and as well as purchase behavior across gender, age, and loyalty status.

## 🛠Tools Used
- MySQL Workbench
- Power BI

## 🔎Key Findings
- The business generated a revenue of $4.4M and $1.7M in profit across the 2-year period, selling 525K units to 50K customers.
- The loyalty program shows lack of purchase edge as loyalty members split almost 50/50 with non-members.
- The Dark 50% and Truffle 80% chocolate types lead by revenue, while Ferrero and Cadbury dominate by volume, outselling brands like Hershey.
- The monthly revenue is volatile, not seasonal. The sharp spikes (August 2023, March 2024) suggest promotions rather than steady growth.
- Adults and seniors drive most purchase while youths lag behind.
- Orders are split nearly 50/50 by gender.

## 🏹Recommendations
- Re-evaluation of loyalty program is vital as it suggests weak conversion system.
- Consider expanding the product lines for the Dark 50% and Truffle 80% chocolate types as they are the revenue leaders.
- Investigate and replicate what drove the spikes in revenue in August 2023 and March 2024
- Investigate why only 11.36% of youths make purchase, and work on bridging the gap.
